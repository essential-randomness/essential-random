Let’s talk about this blog.

Something clicked recently, when Mastodon’s rel=me feature forced me to conceptualize this as my “experimental” blog, thus leading by the shocking—shocking, I say!—realization that it is indeed such. Indeed (as you might notice by half-baked features, links leading to nowhere, and mostly unfilled project entries), I’m leaving trails for myself of knicks and knacks to explore with time.

The why

In short, the reason I’m building this blog is that there I believe there is a perfect storm brewing. For the first time in history, technology is coming out to make the Dream started by the IndieWeb finally not just possible, but accessible and customizable.

In short: for a there is a great, new type of modern website engine just waiting to to be born, just out of my reach. In a way, this blog is my attempt to carve out “the solution” out of a mishmash of the pieces that other people have seen and worked on. Part of my 2022 is going to be exploring how to make what you see here all fit together, and hopefully sharing some of the building block I’ll inevitably build with all of you.

The goals

  1. Act as a “command center” for your existence on the web: there is a lot of interesting microdata that you might want to propagate about you on the web, and it’s kinda telling that we let link.tree be so popular.
  2. Be born from your content, not be the source of it: a blog should be a layer on top of things you can port around the web.’
  3. Propagate metadata to other website, and have everything be scrapable and targetable
  4. Be easily modifiable at the level someone wants it to be modifiable at. It should never be harder than it can be. Everything should be customizable in a way that’s shareable.
  5. Be easily deployable, with no compromises.

UnifiedJS

My love for MDX is inseperable from my love for the unifiedjs ecosystem (and their organizational structure).

UnifiedJS allows you to apply plugins to stuff. For example, here’s some plugins I could add (and whether I added)

  • Github-flavored markdwon, allowing me to strike through things, and
  • Automatically adiding header links to my things
  • Some TOC thing, though I haven’t found the perfecrt one yet. Will have to write a plugin.
  • A plugin I’ve actually been writing, to reimplement a more flexible version of docusaurs notes.

ContentLayer

I have been somewhat disappointed with the development experience of content layer, and can’t really reccommend it as wholeheartidly as i wish i could, but i still find the idea to be just wha’ts needed. It does two main things:

  1. Puts the content in its own folders, and compiles it for you before serving
  2. Applies the MDX plugins
  3. Makes it easy for you to import the content where you want

Problems:

  • Images are separated from the source
  • Not as fine-grained as I’d like (hard to add plugins only to specific things, for example)
  • Should rely on more portable things like Zod
